The brief.

Kim runs a small Christmas shop in Bungendore she's built up over years — the kind of place where every ornament has a story and the regulars know her by name. She needed a website that did the same job online: warm, considered, easy to use, and good for actual sales.

Not a template. Not a generic e-commerce skin. Something that felt like the shop.

What we built.

01

A website from scratch

Hand-coded, fast, and built around Kim's tone of voice. No bloated platform, no template wrestling — just a clean site that loads quickly and gets out of the way of the products.

02

Interactive snow

A subtle, atmospheric snow effect that drifts gently across the page. Just enough magic to set the mood, never enough to get in the way of someone trying to buy a wreath.

03

Holly — the AI shop assistant

Trained on Kim's stock, her policies, and her warm Aussie tone. Holly chats with customers 24/7, answers questions, and pings Kim's phone when a real buyer is on the site. She's the first live deployment of SayG'day, our AI chat platform for Australian small businesses.

The shop never closes when Holly's around. She handles the late-night browsers and the weekend window-shoppers — and Kim only steps in when there's a real sale on the line.

The approach.

Kim is a busy small business owner, not a tech project manager. So the build was structured around as little of her time as possible: short discovery conversations, drafts she could react to in five minutes on her phone, and a deployment that didn't ask her to learn anything new.

Holly is the part she's most excited about — and rightly so. She's a 24/7 staff member who never gets tired, knows the stock, and politely takes a message when Kim is busy with a real customer. The site captures sales that would otherwise have been lost to a missed message or a closed shop.

What's next.

Holly is the showcase for SayG'day — the AI chat platform built specifically for Australian small businesses. If you're a café, a boutique, a tradie, a clinic, or anything in between, your version of Holly is a few conversations away.
